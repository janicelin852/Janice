Smart Tunnel Light Experience

The company's initial system was built for general street lighting; however, due to the difference between tunnel lighting and general street lighting, the feature of tunnel dimming was the goal of this project.

Based on the different time periods of day and night and the measurement value of the brightness outside the tunnel, the system will automatically detect and modify the tunnel interior lighting to provide appropriate brightness and save energy, cutting the running cost of tunnel lighting.

Goals

  • The new Logic design must be compatible with current projects.

Process

I read some literature, analyzed the present system operating model, and produced a solution despite my lack of experience or knowledge. I discovered several practical challenges and misunderstanding after conversations with project manager and engineers. To finalize the dimming strategy, we ran another competitive study and iterated multiple versions.

Research & Evaluation

Desktop research

According to the "Tunnel Lighting Standard," the length of the tunnel can be separated into Access zone, Threshold zone, Transition zone, Interior zone, and Exit zone to prevent the "black hole effect." The goal of this project is to allow the tunnel lights to vary their brightness based on the luminometer. We understand that each tunnel light must have its own zone, with its own average brightness, and that the brightness of the lights will be modified automatically based on the luminometer's brightness.

Competitive Analysis

Based on competitive analysis, we observed the functions and settings are split according to the different scenarios in the present market. This is consistent with Hick's Law, which states that as the number of possibilities increases, people take longer to make decisions. Users will learn how to work more rapidly by focusing on certain features for specific scenarios.

As a result, we decided to design templates for multiple scenarios in the future to give clients with alternatives. This section needs more staff, and in order to finish it on time, the front-end uses the original UI design as well as the accordance with the original system operating logic.

Design & Iterate

The full functional logic structure has been finalized after extensive conversation with engineers. Initially, design was frequently done in an ideal method, without taking into account all possible scenarios. Even though it is merely a tiny change in the UI, it makes a significant difference in the program.

Final Design

  • User-friendly language and operation.
  • Templates for general and tunnel street lighting.
  • Create functions that have the least influence on the original system.

Take away

Reading a lot of literature and competitive analysis is one of the quickest ways to understand unfamiliar areas, and then sharing and discussing the main topics with partners so that they can be more familiar with the project, and make the information symmetrical with each other, which will help the project progress.

This project is more professional and complicated, with several edge cases which were overlooked. I took advantage of this opportunity to inquire about how the engineers dissected the problem and evaluated the logic of each aspect. It will assist me in making more accurate designs in the future.
