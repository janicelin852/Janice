The Energy Management System(EMS)

The Energy Management System (EMS) takes field energy measurement data, evaluates power demand, and displays it to the user in graphs and charts. It enables customers to remotely monitor and schedule in order to meet energy management, power savings, and cost savings goals.

The product includes three webs service and one app for different users:

  1. Cloud web for field owner
  2. Edge web for field administrator
  3. Deploy web for field lead
  4. Deploy app for field staff

Team

1 PM, 2 Front-End Developers, 2 Back-End Developers

Role

UI/UX Designer

Time

2021-2022

Goals

  • Integrate previous projects and create a SaaS service.
  • Built, redefined, and maintained the design system.

Challenges

  • Understanding the detail business logic of previous projects of company.
  • To create a generic design that suits all previous and feature projects.

Process

Discovery & Ideate

Based on prior project experience, we know what fundamental features are required for IoT products, and we then use competitive analysis to evaluate the current industry position and trends.

Because the bulk of the competitors are B2B, purchasing them for usage is nearly impossible. I explored the product in three ways: the first was to directly register to use the free version, the second was to read the product's user guide if registration was not possible, and the third was to join the product's community to get a firsthand look at current user complaints and screenshots.

Define System Framework

We developed the structure of the entire product, which comprises of three web platform and one app, based on previous experience and competitive study. We supply the system functionalities required by various clients, as defined by their business goals.

Design & Test

We had an ongoing project to test at the time we designed this system. I  analyzed the suggested solutions, chose the best one for implementation, and then gave users access to it. Although this release resolved the operational issues, it caused interface viewing issues. As a result, we revisited the product aim and ultimately opted to provide information in a table format, allowing the user to quickly switch between seeing device specifications.

Design strategy

We completed the design strategy after several design iterations

  • Model of generalized equipment display
  • User's consistent operating pattern
  • Add a plugin function for future growth

Take away

As the system's only designer, I've gotten a better understanding of the Internet of Things (IoT). We must understand the functioning and constraints of the various applications, identify commonalities and contrasts, and create a generic and flexible design.

Aside from being a UI/UX designer, I also try to create Product Requirements Documents (PRDs), which is an additional learning opportunity for me. I needed to learn the languages of designers, developers, and product managers in order to create a document that everyone could understand. Every modification must be recorded when the system is complicated. This document will help in communication and the growth of the scope.
