The Energy Management System(EMS)

The Energy Management System (EMS) collects energy measurement data from the field, analyzes power demand and provides it to the user through graphs and charts. It allows users to monitor and schedule remotely to achieve energy management, power saving and money saving goals.

The product includes three webs service and one app for different users:

  1. Cloud web for field owner
  2. Edge web for field administrator
  3. Deploy web for field leader
  4. Deploy app for field staff

Team

1 PM, 2 Front-End Developers, 2 Back-End Developers

Role

UI/UX Designer

Time

2021-2022

Goals

  • Integrate the company's past projects and develop a SaaS product.
  • Replacement of the design system user interface and framework.

Challenges

  • It’s a big job to developing a general design that fits all past and feature projects.
  • Understanding the business logic of a company's past projects is a new area for us.

Process

Discovery & Ideate

We know what basic functions are needed for IoT products based on previous projects' experience, and then we use competitive analysis to understand the current industry status and trends. Because the majority of the competitors are B2B, it is almost Impossible to purchase them for use. I explored the product in three ways: the first was to register directly to use the free version, the second was to read the user guide of the product if registration was not possible, and the third way was to join the product's community to get a firsthand look at current user complaints and screenshots.

Define System Framework

From past experience and competitor analysis, we have defined the framework of the whole product, which consists of three web and one app. We provide the system functions required by different customers, divided by different business goals.

Design ＆ Test

At the time of designing this system, we had an ongoing project to test. We compared the proposed solutions, selected the best one for implementation, and allowed users to operate it. Although this version solved the operational difficulties, but it caused problems with the interface viewing. Therefore, we thought about the product goal again and finally decided to present it in a table format, and allow the user to switch quickly between viewing device details.

Design strategy

After design iterations, we finalized the design strategy as

  • Generalized equipment display model
  • Consistent operation pattern of user
  • Add plugin function for future expansion

Take away

As the solo designer of this system, I have gained a deeper understanding of the Internet of Things (IoT). We need to understand the operation and limitations of the possible applications, find the similarities and differences, and make a universal and flexible design.

In addition to being a UI/UX designer, I also try to write Product Requirements Documents (PRDs), which is another learning opportunity for me. I needed to understand the language of designers, engineers and product managers and write a document that everyone could understand.When the system is complex, every change must be documented. This document will help with communication and scope development.
